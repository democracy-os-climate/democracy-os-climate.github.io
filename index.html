DemocracyOS, what is this?

DemocracyOS is an Open Source Platform to argue, debate and vote on specific political issues, at a time when the internet has changed almost every aspects of everyday life, except for democracies.

Invented in 2012 by Argentinian developers and political scientists, the software is now being freely replicated all over the world.

Paris Climate 2015, what about it?

“For a universal climate agreement”

...says the banner of the French Government's COP21 website.

Indeed, the 21st Conference of the Parties is going to gather 195 worldwide representatives from November 30th to December 11th 2015. These Parties are committed to sign a universal climate agreement. To do so, they are working all year long on a draft meant to be negotiated and adopted at Paris Climate 2015.

Yet if this draft aims to be universal, then it must be recognized as such by the Parties' constituencies.

DemocracyOS Paris Climate 2015, what for?

To get 195 different Parties to adopt a universal climate agreement is a very complex task for the representatives as it is for their constituencies. To bring together 195 different conditions of survival is not that simple.

Nonetheless, we think that this complexity shall not stop us. We want to become more sensitive with the issues at stake by experimenting collectively with the options listed in the draft and make visible the plurality of ways to represent a climate changing world.

Therefore we imagined DemocracyOS and Climate issues becoming opportunities for each other.

Join the experiment

  • providing a non national platform to argue, debate and vote on international agreements and become more sensitive about the institutional responses to climate change.
  • promoting a collective reading of the COP21 Negotiating Draft and bring it closer to the pluralities of our everyday lives.
  • sharing the experience with the other DemocracyOS users because sometimes the content may be as Open Source as the code.
